Physician

In order to be a licensed physician you need to work very hard. The education required is undergraduate school and graduate school in medicine.

In most circumstances to be qualified for medical school you must meet many requirements. You need a great GPA, a great MCAT score, and many prerequisites. Different schools require different prerequisites, but the most common are the following: physics, calculus, biology, general chemistry, and organic chemistry.

The MCAT

The Medical College Admission Test(MCAT) is a standardized test that medical schools use to screen applicants and predict a students success in medical school and their field of medicine. In other words, it is just like the SAT's for medical school.

The MCAT consists of many different subjects. About 50% of the content is not science. The scientific subjects consist of Biology, General Chemistry, Organic Chemistry, Biochemistry, and Physics. The rest includes Sociology, Psychology, and Critical Analysis and Reasoning Skills(CARS). CARS makes up about a fourth of the whole exam.
